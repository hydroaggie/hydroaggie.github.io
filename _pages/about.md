Welcome to the Shuai Computational and Integrated Hydrology (**SCI-Hy**) group at Utah State University, led by [Pin Shuai](/members/Pin_Shuai)! We investigate the complex and dynamic hydrological system through innovative computational and field-based approaches. Our work is driven by a commitment to advancing scientific knowledge, fostering innovation, and addressing pressing environmental challenges.

Our research spans three interconnected areas: 1) **hydrological and biogeochemical processes** at the aquatic–terrestrial interface, with a focus on **groundwater–surface water interactions**; 2) **flow and transport of heat, nutrients, and contaminants from bedrock to canopy** across watersheds, including feedbacks among hydrology, land use, and climate change; and 3) **human–water interactions** and their implications for water security. We seek to answer the following questions: Where does groundwater come from? How does groundwater move across the landscape and interact with surface water? What does this exchange mean for water quality and availability?

We apply a model–data integrative approach that combines **field**, **laboratory**, and **numerical modeling** techniques to advance understanding of hydrological processes. We are strong advocates for **open-source** and **reproducible** scientific research. For current and past projects, see the [Projects](/projects) page.

We are always looking for passionate and talented individuals to join our team. Visit the [Prospective Students](/prospective_students) page for more information.
